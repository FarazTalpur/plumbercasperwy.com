Frequently Asked Questions

Test your sump pump every three months by pouring water into the sump pit to ensure it activates and drains properly.

Regularly check the sump pit for debris, clean the pump inlet screen, check the power cord for wear, and test the backup battery if applicable.

Pour water into the sump pit until the float activates the pump. Ensure water is being discharged properly and listen for any unusual noises.

Replace your sump pump every 7 to 10 years or sooner if it shows signs of wear, such as making unusual noises, failing to start, or running continuously.

Signs include unusual noises, constant running, failure to start, and irregular cycling, as well as visible rust or wear on the parts.
