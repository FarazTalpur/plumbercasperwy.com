Frequently Asked Questions

A slow-draining sink is often caused by a clog in the drain. You can attempt to clear it using a plunger or a drain snake. If the clog persists, consider using a chemical drain cleaner or contacting a plumber for further assistance.

To prevent a leaking showerhead, make sure it is properly tightened and has a working washer inside the connection. Regularly clean it to remove mineral build-up which can also cause leaking, and replace any worn-out washers or seals as needed.

A continuously running toilet could be due to a faulty flapper, a broken fill valve, or the float being set too high. Check the flapper for wear and replace if necessary, adjust the float, or replace the fill valve to resolve the issue.

Low water pressure can be caused by sediment build-up in pipes, blockages in aerators, or issues with the local water supply. Clean or replace clogged aerators, check for and clear any pipe blockages, and ensure your main water valve is fully open.

A leaking bathroom faucet is often due to worn-out washers or seals. Turn off the water supply, disassemble the faucet to access the components, and replace any damaged washers or seals. Reassemble the faucet and turn the water back on to check for leaks.
