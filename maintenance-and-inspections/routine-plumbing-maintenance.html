Frequently Asked Questions

Routine plumbing maintenance should be done at least once a year to ensure your plumbing system is functioning efficiently and to prevent any major issues.

Common signs include low water pressure, slow drainage, unusual noises like banging pipes, and increased water bills without a corresponding increase in usage.

Routine tasks include checking for leaks, inspecting water heaters, testing water pressure, cleaning drains, and ensuring that pipes are insulted and clear from obstructions.

While there are some maintenance tasks homeowners can do themselves, such as cleaning drains and checking for visible leaks, a professional plumber can perform more comprehensive checks and repairs.

To prevent plumbing issues, avoid pouring grease down the drain, use strainers to catch hair and debris, regularly clean out pipes and traps, and keep an eye out for any signs of wear or leaks.
