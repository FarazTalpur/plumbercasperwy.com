Frequently Asked Questions

To prevent outdoor pipes from freezing, use pipe insulation or heat tape. Additionally, ensure outdoor faucets are turned off and drained before freezing temperatures arrive, and consider installing frost-proof spigots.

If your outdoor faucet is leaking, check if the packing nut is loose and tighten it. If the leak persists, it might be due to a worn washer or damaged valve seat, and you may need to replace these components.

To detect leaks in outdoor plumbing, look for signs like unexplained puddles near pipes, a reduction in water pressure, or an unusually high water bill. You can also listen for the sound of water running when all taps are off.

Copper, PVC, and PEX are commonly used materials for outdoor plumbing. Copper is durable and long-lasting, PVC is cost-effective and resistant to corrosion, and PEX is flexible and resistant to freezing.

To increase water pressure in your outdoor hose bib, first, check for any blockages or leaks in the hose or pipe leading to the bib. Inspect and clean the bib's aerator and ensure the water supply valve is fully open. If the issue persists, you may need to consult a plumber to assess the plumbing system.
